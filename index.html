Located in the heart of Mallow, St. Mary‚Äôs Church is a cornerstone of the community and a landmark of Irish religious architecture.

The church we see today was erected in 1818. It was designed to serve the growing population of Mallow during a period of prosperity. Its distinctive T-plan layout and Romanesque features make it one of the most significant 19th-century buildings in North Cork.

The interior is equally breathtaking, featuring a grand arcade supported by massive columns, leading the eye towards the sanctuary. The marble memorials along the walls act as a silent archive, documenting the lives of influential Mallow families and historical figures over the last two centuries.
