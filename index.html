Cork Heritage Project

SAINT
MARY'S

Located in the heart of Mallow, St. Mary’s Church is a cornerstone of the community and a landmark of Irish religious architecture.

The church we see today was erected in 1818. Its T-plan layout and Romanesque features make it a significant 19th-century building in North Cork.

The interior features a grand arcade supported by massive columns, leading to the sanctuary. Marble memorials document the lives of influential Mallow families.

Architectural Highlights

  • •Italianate-style limestone bell tower
  • •Elegant Corinthian interior columns
  • •Exquisite stained glass and marblework
